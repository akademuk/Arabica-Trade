green coffee

Green coffee is a story of culture, history, and potential. It embodies opportunities for creating diverse coffee flavors and continues to astound people worldwide. It is a precious raw material, serving as a starting point for one of the most popular beverages in the world. The beans are obtained through a special processing of the sweet pulp of the coffee tree’s fruit

A BRIEF HISTORY

The history of green coffee traces back to ancient times. It all began in east africa, where residents used coffee berries for making beverages for centuries. Legend has it that a shepherd named kaldi discovered how the coffee plant invigorated his flock. This marked the beginning of the discovery of the magic of coffee. Coffee Varieties

Arabica

Arabica is a key coffee variety grown on an industrial scale. It requires specific conditions to thrive: elevations between 600 and 2,500 meters and intensive care. This variety dominates in Central and South America, as well as in many East African countries, and is considered the source of the highest-quality coffee

Robusta

Robusta thrives in warm tropical climates, is more resistant to weather conditions, and contains more caffeine. It is easier to cultivate and highly adaptable to different environments, making it a popular choice for blends and instant coffee production
